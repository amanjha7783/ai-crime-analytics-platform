# PowerPoint Source

## Slide 1: Title

AI-Driven Crime Analytics and Visualization Platform for Karnataka State Police Datathon 2026.

## Slide 2: Problem

Fragmented police records make it difficult to detect crime patterns, identify repeat offenders, analyze criminal networks, predict hotspots, and support proactive policing.

## Slide 3: Solution

An intelligence platform combining ETL, PostGIS, machine learning, explainability, FastAPI, and a Next.js police command dashboard.

## Slide 4: Data Pipeline

Collect crime data, validate, clean, handle missing values, engineer features, run EDA, store in PostgreSQL/PostGIS, and feed ML models.

## Slide 5: ML Intelligence

Crime classification, hotspot prediction, repeat offender detection, risk scoring, trend forecasting, anomaly detection, and explainable AI.

## Slide 6: Dashboard

KPI dashboard, interactive map-ready views, heatmaps, crime trends, criminal network graph, risk scores, and reports.

## Slide 7: Architecture

Police data flows through ETL to PostGIS and ML pipelines, then through FastAPI to the intelligence portal.

## Slide 8: Impact

The platform helps officers prioritize patrols, detect repeat activity, compare districts, and generate actionable intelligence reports.

## Slide 9: Deployment

Docker for local deployment, Vercel for frontend, Render or Railway for backend, PostgreSQL/PostGIS for spatial storage, and Redis for caching.

## Slide 10: Future Scope

Secure official data feeds, advanced model training, SHAP explanations, streaming alerts, and investigator workflows.
